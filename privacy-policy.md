# Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** July 3, 2026

Explain This is a Chrome extension that explains text you explicitly select or
enter. This policy describes the extension's Built-in AI mode, optional Bring
Your Own Key (BYOK) mode, and local browser features.

## Data processed

### Built-in AI mode

When you request an explanation, the selected or entered text, response language,
and explanation style are sent over HTTPS to the Explain This backend. The backend
forwards the request to its configured AI provider and streams the explanation
back to the extension.

The application does **not** write the original selected text or generated
explanation to its backend database. Request content is handled transiently in
memory for the time needed to provide the explanation.

To enforce the free allowance and detect abuse, the backend processes:

- a random installation identifier generated by the extension;
- the request's IP address, which is immediately combined with a private salt and
  hashed for a network-level abuse backstop;
- daily and per-minute request counts; and
- cooldown and quota-reset timestamps.

The rate-limit store contains hashes and counters, not selected text, generated
explanations, raw API keys, or browsing history. Rate-limit state is deleted
31 days after the last activity for that anonymous bucket.

Infrastructure providers may process normal delivery metadata such as timestamps,
IP addresses, status codes, and traffic volume under their own service terms. We
configure application logging not to include selected text or response bodies.

### Bring Your Own Key mode

BYOK is an optional Advanced feature. Your API key is stored only in
`chrome.storage.local` on your device. Requests in BYOK mode go directly from the
extension to the provider you selected (OpenAI, Google Gemini, or DeepSeek) and do
not pass through the Explain This backend.

### Data stored locally

The extension may store the following in `chrome.storage.local`:

- language, explanation style, AI mode, and enabled-site preferences;
- a random installation identifier used for anonymous quota enforcement;
- optional provider API keys;
- anonymous local request and estimated-token counters; and
- if **Save recent explanations** is enabled, up to 10 recent items and 100 cached
  explanations.

Save recent explanations is visible in Settings and can be turned off. Turning it
off clears the locally stored history and explanation cache. Uninstalling the
extension also removes its local storage through Chrome.

## Site access and browsing activity

Explain This does not track or store browsing history. By default, its webpage
selection script runs only after you invoke the extension on the active tab.
You may optionally grant persistent access to specific domains under **Enabled
sites**. Those permissions can be removed from Settings at any time.

Only text you actively submit is sent for explanation. Do not submit passwords,
authentication codes, private keys, payment information, or other sensitive data.

## Retention and use

- Selected text and generated explanations are not persisted by the Explain This
  backend application.
- Anonymous quota and abuse counters are deleted 31 days after the last activity.
- Local settings, keys, and optional history remain until you clear them or
  uninstall the extension.
- Operational metadata retained by infrastructure providers follows their account
  configuration and applicable service terms.

We do not sell personal data. We do not use selected text or explanations to train
our own models. AI and infrastructure providers may process data under their own
terms and policies.

## Service providers

Built-in AI mode uses:

- **Cloudflare Workers and Durable Objects** for secure request relay and anonymous
  quota enforcement; and
- the AI provider configured by Explain This for response generation.

BYOK mode uses the provider you choose directly. Provider selection for Built-in
AI may change without an extension update. Review the applicable third-party
privacy terms before submitting sensitive or confidential material.

## Your choices

You can:

- disable and clear recent explanations in Settings;
- remove any domain under Enabled sites;
- clear a saved BYOK credential;
- switch between Built-in AI and BYOK; or
- uninstall the extension to remove all local extension data.

Because free-mode quota records are anonymous and contain no account profile or
request content, we may need your installation identifier and approximate request
time to investigate a deletion request.
